"Silent Spring" is a book written by marine biologist Rachel Carson and published in 1962. It is considered a pillar of the environmental conservation movement and played a significant role in creating public awareness of the impact of pesticides on the environment and human health. The book examines the negative effects of pesticides, especially DDT, on ecosystems and human health. Carson presented scientific evidence of how these chemicals can affect wildlife, including birds and other animals, as well as the impact on natural resources such as water and soil. She argued that the extensive use of pesticides can have serious and unexpected consequences, including poisoning food chains and the emergence of resistant species. "Silent Spring" sparked debate around the world and led to significant changes in pesticide regulatory policies. The book helped launch the environmental conservation movement and promote the idea of protecting nature and human health in the face of uncontrolled use of chemicals.
